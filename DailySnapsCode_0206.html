GamePop or GameStop?
You know the old saying: always leave the party when you’re having the most fun. Well, it seems GameStoppers are considering following that advice if things don’t get better soon.
GameStop shares popped nearly 9% in extended trading on Tuesday before paring most of those gains, following a Q1 report that was worse than expected with a LPS of $2.08 on revenues that were up 8% at $1.38bn, though the company said it’s still not comfortable giving guidance yet.
Hardware and accessories sales fell over 4% YoY, which is a big deal considering those metrics make up the bulk of the business’ revenues. On the plus side, sales of collectibles rose, and software sales are on the up and up, plus the company brought in $76.9m in proceeds from the sale of digital items. Speaking of which…
“Digital assets are core to the future of gaming”, said CEO Matt Furlong as he told investors that GameStop would be investing heavily in its blockchain efforts after launching a crypto wallet in May, though we didn’t get a financial update on the brand’s highly anticipated NFT marketplace.

Earnings get investors’ tails wagging
Ryan Cohen’s pet retailer gives investors a sweet serving of Q1 earnings, and they hungrily come back for more after seeing guidance.
Chewy stock soared up to 25% in extended trading on Wednesday after reporting a surprise profit and smashing estimates with EPS of $0.04 on revenues that have more than doubled since its 2019 IPO, to hit $2.43bn – though shares are still trading around their lowest levels since said IPO.
Gross margins were in the spotlight after this earnings season has highlighted the metric as deeply feeling the impact of crunching supply chains and rising inflation. Margins only declined ten basis points compared to last year to come in at 27.5% – a pleasant surprise that’s been helped by Chewy’s pricing power and continued demand.
Macroeconomic headwinds make “accurate forecasting difficult”, but the brand still expects the pet food market to remain resilient and forecasts FY net sales growth of up to 17%, earning a tail wag from investors. That being said, short-selling on the stock remains prominent with 23.5% of the available float sold short recently.

Binance breaks the bank
Binance Labs fills its web3 piggy bank, aiming to “accelerate the mass adoption of the blockchain technology and crypto”.
Binance Labs has raised a $500m fund that’s set to support novel projects across the web3 space – be it based in DeFi, NFTs, gaming, or social platforms. The fund managed to pull in global investors including DST Global Partners and Breyer Capital, despite the markets looking pretty bearish rn.
That’s a lotta money. Remind me, what’s web3 again? The ELI5 explanation: it’s the internet, with less centralization – using blockchain tech to improve user experiences and efficiency.
Only last week did we see a $4.5bn fund for crypto startups, all thanks to Andreessen Horowitz’s far-reaching influence. Despite the news, Binance’s BNB token is still trading down 42% since the start of the year.

Sheryl says see ya
Mark Zuckerberg is saying goodbye to one of his closest lieutenants and Meta loses one of its long-standing key execs.
Sheryl Sandberg is stepping down as the Chief Operating Officer of Meta after 14 years at the helm, representing a major shakeup of the brand’s leadership team. Sandberg will remain on the board after leaving in the fall, but – honestly – investors seem to have been expecting her departure for a while now.
Even so, shares slumped 5% in a late day decline to close down 2.58% on the day, and it's worth noting that Meta’s stock is down over 51% from its September high, which is pretty much when it announced its historic rebrand – worse than the Nasdaq’s 26% decline in the same period.
Meta will take its final rebranding step on June 9, when its trading name will finally take on a new title under the ticker META – they initially wanted to trade under MVRS but that ticker was taken already. Soz Zuck. Hopefully the rest of its transition into virtual reality goes a bit smoother.

A new era for Zcash?
Network Upgrade 5 goes live on Zcash’s mainnet, dubbed by devs as a really, really BFD (big f*cking deal).
Network Upgrade 5 (NU5) launched this week, becoming the first major upgrade Zcash has had since November 2020. The launch will allow for private and trustless digital payments on mobile phones through its Halo proving system – an open-source model aiming to simplify the complexity required to verify private transactions.
Zcash says this is “the most important milestone” since its crypto launched in 2016. For those that aren’t aware – it’s like Bitcoin but with a focus on privacy. Indeed, it’s a fork of the OG crypto and even has the same coin supply of 21m.
Despite the historic upgrade, Zcash is actually down 3% since Monday. Worse yet, it’s down to trade below where it was before its 100% run-up in March. *raised eyebrow emoji*
